# Sentences

1. True heroes are not afraid to make tough choices.
   - *make* is a standard **Monotransitive** that means "*to perform*" or "*to execute*", rather than its causative "*to force/change*" meaning.
   - "*to make tough choices*" is an **Adjective Complement**. Some adjectives (like *afraid, proud, lucky, eager*) feel "*incomplete*" on their own. They need an *Infinitive Phrase* to explain what the subject is afraid/proud/lucky of.
   - *not* is a Negative Particle.

2. True heroes are not afraid of making tough choices.
   - This is almost the same sentence as before and it conveys the same meaning, but this time the part "*of making tough choices*" is a Prepositional Phrase rather than an Infinitive Phrase. But the phrase remains as an **Adjective Complement**.

3. Tough choices make heroes brave.
   - *make* is a Complex Transitive Verb.
   - *brave* is an **Object Complement, a Resultative, Dynamic adjective**

4. I painted the walls yellow.
   - *painted* is a Complex Transitive Verb.
   - *yellow* is an **Object Complement, a Resultative, Stative adjective**

5. I found the walls yellow.
   - The analysis can be done in 2 ways.
     - If you mean "I judged them", then *found* is a **Complex Transitive Verb** and *yellow* is an **Object Complement** and an **Depictive Complement**.
     - If you mean "I located them", then *found* is a **Monotransitive Verb** and yellow is a **Physical Depictive Adjunct**.

6. She left the door open.
   - *left* is a Complex Transitive Verb and it functions as a **causative/stative bridge**.
   - *open* is the **Object Complement**, and a Resultative and Stative.

7. The new rules made my job harder.
   - *made* is a **CTV**.
   - *harder* is the **Object Complement**, a **Resultative and a Comparative adjective**.
   - *my* is a **Central Possessive Determiner**.

8. He made me clean my room.

   - *made* is a **Complex Transitive Verb**
   - *me* is the Direct Object
   - "*clean my room*" is the **Object Complement and Verb Phrase** (**Bare Infinitive**)

9. I watched the sun setting.

   - *watched* is a **CTV**. Specifically, **a Verb of Perception**.
   - "*the sun*" is a **Direct Object** and **Noun Phrase**.
   - *setting* is the **Object Complement and a Present Participle**
     - Since you are using the Present Participle instead of the Past Participle (*I watched the sun set*), the focus is **on the process of the sun going down**, not just the finished result. Also, this form suggests you caught it **in the middle** of the action and did not watch it from top to bottom.

10. "I saw him cleaning his room." and "I saw him clean his room" 
    - Both sentences are correct and their analysis is pretty similar, the only difference is that the first sentence uses a **Present Participle Verb Phrase as an Object Complement**; meanwhile the second sentences uses a **Bare Infinitive Phrase as an Object Complement**.

11. "I saw the room clean." and "I saw the room cleaned"
    - Both sentences are correct, but the meaning and semantic change a bit.
      In both sentences, the verb *saw* is a **CTV**, but the **Object Complement part is different**
      - *clean* is a pure adjective, in this case, the **Object Complement is a Physical Depictive Complement, it is a Stative Adjective**. 
        The sentence is equivalent to "I found the room clean".
        You perceived a State.
      - *cleaned* is a **Passive Action, a Past Participle**.
        The passive part (-ed) captures that "acted-upon" quality, so someone *cleaned* the room. 
        Unlike the adjective *clean* (which is just a quality), *cleaned* implies that the event of cleaning has taken place. The process was done to the room.
        You perceived a Passive Action, the result of it.

12. A door opens in your mind when you learn this.

    - *opens* is an **Unaccusative Verb**, a **Verb of Motion/Change**.  It is also an **Ergative verb**, the **Intransitive (Unaccusative)** side of this verb.
    - "*in your mind*" is a **Verb Adjunct**, and **Adjunct of Place** (**Locative**). It is also a **Prepositional Phrase**.
    - "*when you learn this*" is a **Temporal Subordinate Clause**
      - *when* is the **Subordinating Conjunction**. It links the dependent clause to the main clause.
      - *you* is the **Subject/pronoun**
      - *learn* is a **Monotransitive Verb**.
      - *this* is the Direct Object and Demonstrative Pronoun. The thing being learned.

    <u>Note</u>: A note for "*in your mind*". The phrase is a **Verb Adjunct**, it is not mandatory. Compare this to a **Verb Complement** (specifically a **Locative Complement**), where the location is mandatory for the verb to function:

    - I put the book **on the table**. 
      *Test*: "I put the book" (This fails; the sentence is incomplete because *put* requires a location)

    <u>Note</u>: A note for *Adjuncts*. **Adjuncts of Place or Time** are used often:

    - The meeting started **at 9:00 AM**. (*Adjunct of Time*)

    - The equipment was installed **at the site**. (*Adjunct of Place*)

      And because they're *adjuncts*, you can move them around for emphasis:

    - **At the site**, the equipment was installed (This still works because adjuncts are flexible!)

13. The mineral deposits reside under the surface.

    - *The mineral deposits* is the subject and NP.
      - *The* is the central determiner
      - mineral is an **Attributive Adjective**. It modifies deposits.
    - *reside* is an **Intransitive Verb**. Specifically, it's a **Verb of Residence/Position**.
    - "*under the surface*" is a **Locative Complement** (A type of **Verb Complement**). It is also a **Prepositional Phrase**.

14. The workers placed the equipment inside the shaft.

    - "*inside the shaft*" is a **Locative Complement**, a **PP** and an **Adverbial of Place** (*Functional label*).
    - *placed* is a **CTV**.

15. The workers repaired the equipment inside the shaft.

    - *repaired* is a **Monotransitive Verb**.
    - *the equipment* is the **Direct Object**.
    - *inside the shaft* is a **Locative Adjunct** (*Structural label*), a **Prepositional Phrase** and an **Adverbial of Place** (*Functional label*)

16. The workers extracted the ore carefully last night

    - *extracted* is a **Monotransitive Verb**.
    - *carefully* is a **Verb Adjunct** (*Structural*) and an **Adverbial of Manner** (*Functional*).
    - "*last night*" is also a **Verb Adjunct** (*Structural*) and an **Adverbial of Time** (*Functional*). It is also a **Noun Phrase**.
      - *last* is a **post-determiner/adjective**
      - *night* is the head of the **NP**.

    <u>Note</u>: **Ordinals** like *first, last, next* or **Cardinals** like *one, two* come after the central determiner but before standard descriptive adjectives, so they fill out the position of **Post-determiner**.

17. The workers extracted the ore during the night shift.

    - *extracted* is a **Monotransitive Verb**
    - "*during the night shift*" is a **Verb Adjunct**, an **Adverbial of Time and a PP**
    - *night* is a **Attributive Noun** or **Noun Adjunct**

18. The shift lasted until midnight.

    - *lasted* is an **Intransitive Verb**.
    - "*until midnight*" is a **Temporal Locative Complement**, **Prepositional Phrase** and **Adverbial of Time**

19. The supervisor kept the records in the office

    - *kept* is a **CTV**
    - "*in the office*" is a **Locative Complement, a Place Locative, and a Prepositional Phrase**, and an **Adverbial of Place**.

20. He baked the bread brown.

    - *baked* is a **CTV**.
    - *brown* is a **Resultative Adjective** and **Object Complement**.

   

21. Look at the room.
    - The sentence is **Imperative**. The subject "*You*" is implied (the *hidden* subject)
    - "*Look at*" is a **Transitive Phrasal Verb** (specifically a **Prepositional Verb**). It is inseparable
22. All the workers extracted the ore.
    - "*All the workers*" is the **subject and NP**
    - *All* is a **Pre-Determiner**, *the* is a **Central Determiner**, *workers* is the **head of the NP**
23. All of the workers extracted the ore.
    - "*All of the workers*" is the **Subject, NP and a Quantifying Partitive**
    - *All* is the **head of the NP, a pronoun and a quantifier**
    - "*of the workers*" is an **Adjectival Post-Modifier and a PP**

24. Two tons of ore were processed.

    - "*Two tons of ore*" is the **Subject, NP and Measure Partitive**
      - *Two* is a **Central Determiner** (specifically a **Cardinal Numeral**), and a **Pre-Modifier** (Quantifies the Head)
      - *tons* is the **Head of the NP**
      - "*of ore*" is a **PP and an Adjectival Post-Modifier**
    - "*were processed*" is a **Passive Verb Phrase**
      - *were* is the **Auxiliary Verb** (Passive Marker)
      - *processed* is the **Past Participle** (Main verb)

    <u>Note</u>: There's a rule:
    If you said "The two tons", then *the* is the **Central Determiner** and *two* is a **Post-Determiner**. But since *Two* is standing alone at the front, it takes the Central slot.
