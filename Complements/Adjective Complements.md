# Adjective Complements

They are mandatory (or highly expected) partner for an adjective. It "completes" the meaning of the adjective.

An Adjective Complement is almost always a phrase or a clause that follows the adjective to explain **what** or **why** that adjectives applies.



## Infinitive Phrase

- True heroes are not afraid **to make tough choices**. (Explains the action the subject is afraid of)



## Prepositional Phrase

- True heroes are not afraid **of making tough choices**. (*Explains the action the subject is afraid of*)

- She is very good **at math**. (*Explains the area she is good in*.)



## Present Participle Phrase (-Ing Phrase)

- This tool is busy **drilling the hole**. (*Explains the activity the tool is busy with*)



## That-Clause (also called Noun Clauses or Content Clauses or Complement Clauses)

- I am sure **that he will arrive**.
  - Explains the fact I am sure of.
