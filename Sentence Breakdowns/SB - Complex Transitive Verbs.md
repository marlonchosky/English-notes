1. Tough choices make heroes brave
   - Tough choices: The subject and NP
   - make: finite verb and CTV (Complex Transitive Verb).
   - heroes: DO
   - brave: OC (Object Complement) and Resultative Adjectival Phrase.
2. Tough choices make heroes very brave
   - Same as before
   - very brave: OC and Resultative Adjectival Phrase
     - very: Pre-modifier (Adverb / Intensifier)
3. Tough choices make heroes brave enough to face the fire
   - Same as before
   - brave [complement]: OC and Adjectival Phrase
   - brave: head of the Adjective Phrase
   - enough: Post-modifier, Degree Adverb
   - to face the fire: Adjective Complement, and Infinitive Phrase (completes the meaning of "brave enough")
4. Tough choices make heroes act
   - Tough choices: Subject and NP
   - make: CTV
   - heroes: DO
   - act: Verbal Complement (a Bare Infinitive) and its agent is "heroes"
5. It gets the job done
   - It: Subject and Pronoun
   - gets: CTV
   - the job: DO
   - done: **Verbal Complement, Past Participle in the Passive Voice** (the Active Voice would be "It gets him to do the job")
6. I painted the walls yellow
   - painted: CTV
   - the walls: DO and NP
   - yellow: OC and adjective, a Resultative adjective
7. I found the walls yellow
   - found: CTV
   - the walls: DO
   - yellow: OC and Depictive Adjective
8. I found it boring
   - found: CTV
   - it: DO
   - boring: OC, Depictive Adjective and Participial Adjective
9. I found the dog alive
   - If you mean "I located the dog"
     - found: Monotransitive
     - the dog: DO and NP
     - alive: Depictive Adjunct Adjective
   - If you mean "I discovered that the dog was alive"
     - found: CTV
     - the dog: DO and NP
     - alive: OC and Depictive Adjective
10. She left the door open
   - left: CTV
   - the door: DO and NP
   - open: OC and Resultative Adjective
11. The new rules made my job harder
    - The new rules: Subject and NP
    - made: CTV
    - my job: DO and NP
    - harder: OC and Resultative Comparative Adjective
12. He made me clean my room
    - made: CTV
    - me: DO and Pronoun
    - clean my room: Verbal Complement Phrase, Bare Infinitive, its agent is "me"
    - my room: DO and NP
    - my: Possessive Determiner
13. I watched the sun setting
    - watched: CTV
    - the sun: DO and NP
    - setting: Verb Complement, Present Participle Verb
14. I saw him clean his room / I saw him cleaning his room
    - Both sentences are almost similar, but the first uses a Bare Infinitive Phrase and the second one uses a Present Participial Phrase.  
15. I saw the room clean
    - saw: CTV
    - the room: DO and NP
    - clean: OC and Depictive Adjective
16. I saw the room cleaned
    - saw: CTV
    - the room: DO and NP
    - cleaned: Verbal Complement, Past Participle and in the Passive Voice
17. The workers placed the equipment inside the shaft.
    - placed: CTV
    - the equipment: DO and NP
    - inside the shaft: Locative Adverbial Complement, and a PP
      - inside: Head word
      - the shaft: Object of the Preposition
18. The supervisor kept the records in the office
    - The supervisor: Subject and NP
    - kept: CTV
    - the records: DO and NP
    - in the office: Locative Adverbial Complement, and PP 
19. Keep it to yourself
    - Keep: CTV
    - it: DO
    - to yourself: Locative Adverbial Complement and Secondary Predicate (it is a metaphorical place)
      - Secondary Predicate: It [is] to yourself (**it is telling the assigned boundary**)
20. He baked the bread brown
    - baked: CTV
    - the bread: DO and NP
    - brown: OC and Adjectival Phrase and a Resultative Objective Secondary Predicate
