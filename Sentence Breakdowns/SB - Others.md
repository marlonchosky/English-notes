1. The workers repaired the equipment inside the shaft.

   - If you intend to mean that the repairment happened inside the shaft:

     - repaired: Monotransitive

     - the equipment: DO and NP

     - inside the shaft: Adverbial Adjunct of Place

   - If you intend to mean that the equipment was inside the shaft during the repairment

     - repaired: Monotransitive
     - the equipment inside the shaft: DO and NP
     - equipment: head word of the NP
     - inside the shaft: Adjectival Post-Modifier

   - If you intend that the repairment happened, and during this the equipment was inside the shaft

     - repaired: Monotransitive
     - the equipment: DO and NP
     - inside the shaft: Depictive Secondary Predicate

2. The workers extracted the ore carefully last night

   - extracted: Monotransitive
   - the ore: DO and NP
   - carefully: Adverbial Adjunct of Manner
   - last night: Adverbial Adjunct of Time, and NP
     - last: Post-Determiner / Adjective (**Deictic Adjective**-it points to a time relative to now))
     - night: head word

   <u>==Note==</u>: Saying "the last night" instead of "last night" changes the expression from a Deictic expression to a Relative expression. Adding "the", the expression could mean to the last night of a 10-day project, referring to the day 10, regardless of when you are telling the story. 

3. The workers extracted the ore during the night shift.

   - extracted: Monotransitive
   - the ore: DO and NP
   - during the night shift: Adverbial Adjunct of Time and a PP
     - during: head word 
     - the night shift: Object of the prep. and NP
     - the: Central determiner
     - night: Noun Adjunct
     - shift: Head word of the NP
