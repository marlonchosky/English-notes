1. The river froze solid
   - The river: Subject and NP
   - froze: Unaccusative and Secondary CIV
   - solid: Secondary Predicate / Predicative Adjunct, and a Resultative Adjective
     - It is a Secondary Predicate because you can  see the Invisible Sentence
       - **Primary Predicate**: The river froze
       - **Secondary Predicate**: The river [is] solid
2. The vase broke into pieces
   - The vase: Subject and NP
   - broke: Unaccusative and Secondary CIV
   - into pieces: Secondary Predicate / Resultative Predicative Adjunct and PP
     - Primary Predicate: The vase broke
     - Secondary Predicate: The vase is in pieces.
3. The river glowed blue.
   - glowed: Unaccusative and Secondary CIV
   - blue: Secondary Predicate / Predicative Adjunct, and Depictive Adjective
     - Primary Predicate: The river glowed
     - Secondary Predicate: The river was blue
4. He broke the vase **into pieces**
   - broke: Monotransitive Verb
   - the vase: DO and NP
   - into pieces: Secondary Predicate / Resultative Predicative Adjunct, and a PP
     - Secondary Predicate: The vase is in pieces (**Dynamic to Static Shift**)
5. He ate the pizza **cold**.

   - The pizza [was] cold.
   - ate: Monotransitive Verb
   - cold: Secondary Predicate / Depictive Predicative Adjunct (Object-Oriented), and an Adjective Phrase
6. He died **a hero**
   - died: Unaccusative and Secondary CIV
   - a hero: Secondary Predicate and Depictive Predicative Adjunct, and NP
7. He wiped the table **to a shine**.
   - wiped: Monotransitive Verb
   - the table: DO and NP
   - to a shine: Secondary Predicate and Resultative Predicative Adjunct, and a PP
     * Secondary Predicate: The table is shiny
     * to: head word of the PP
     * a shine: Object of the Preposition and NP
8. He beat the metal **into a flat sheet** 
   - beat: Monotransitive Verb
   - the metal: DO and NP
   - into a flat sheet: Secondary Predicate and Resultative Predicative Adjunct, and a PP
     - Secondary Predicate: The metal is now a flat sheet
     - into: Head word of the PP
     - a flat sheet: Object of the Preposition and NP
