# Sentences

1. The number of miners is 50.

   - *The* is a **Central Determiner**
   - *number* is the **head of the NP**
   - "*of miners*" is a **PP, and an Adjectival Post-Modifier**
   - *50* is a **Noun and Subject Complement**

2. This is where I was last time

   - *This* is a **Demonstrative Pronoun**.
   - *is* is a **Linking Verb**.
   - "*where I was last time*" is a **Noun Clause and a Subject Complement**
   - *where* is the **Subordinating Conjunction**, but also it is a **Relative Adverb** (*it represents the location*) or **Adverb of Place** (*fronted*)
   - *was* is <span type="incorrect">NOT a Linking Verb</span>, but an **Intransitive (Locative) Verb.**
   - "*last time*" is an **Adverbial of Time, an Adverb Adjunct and a NP**

   <u>Note</u>: Usually, "*was*" is a linking verb (e.g., "*I was tired*"). But when *was* means "**to be located/present**", it is **Intransitive** because it isn't linking the subject to an adjective; it's just stating existence in a place.

3. The tired workers in the office are drinking water.

   - *The* is the **Central Determiner** and **Pre-Modifier**
   - *tired* is an **Attributive Adjective** and a **Pre-Modifier**
   - "*in the office*" is an **Adjectival Post-Modifier and PP**

   <u>Note</u>: If the sentence were "The workers are drinking water **in the office**", then the bold part would be **Adverbial of Place**. But by placing it inside the Subject NP, it is a modifier of the noun.

4. I had the report translated

   - *had* is a **Complex Transitive Verb**, meaning "**to keep/possess in a state**"
   - "*the report*" is the DO and NP
   -  *translated* is the **Object Complement** and a **Resultative Adjective**

   <u>Note</u>: The structure of *have* could've been to use a **verb** as an **Object Complement**. In this case, the meaning would've changed a bit to "I caused the report to be translated"

5. I want the mineral samples tested.

   - *want* is a **CTV**
   - *tested* is an **OC** and a **Resultative Adjective**

6. The manager wants the project finished

   - *wants* is a **CTV**
   - *finished* is a **OC and a Resultative Adjective**

7. You have the structure mapped

   - *have* is a **CTV** indicating possession of a state
   - "*the structure*" is the **DO and a NP**
   - *mapped* is an **Object Complement, a Resultative Adjective**
   - The sentence means **I have the structure + it is mapped**.

8. He bought his brother a car.

   - *bought* is a **Ditransitive Verb**
   - "*his brother*" is the **IO and NP**
   - "a car" is the **DO and NP**

9. He bought a car for his brother

   - *bought* is a **Monotransitive Verb**
   - "*a car*" is the **DO and NP**
   - "*for his brother*" is a **Verb Adjunct, an Adverbial of Beneficiary and a PP**

10. The food turned bad

    - "*The food*" is the **Subject, NP and Patient/Undergoer**
    - *turned* is a **Linking Verb and a Light Unaccusative Verb** (It is light because it needs the adjective)
      - The logic is that the food didn't *turn* (like a car turns a corner); the food **underwent a transition** into the state of being "bad"
    - *bad* is the **Subject Complement, Resultative Adjective Complement**

11. He turned the page

    - *turned* is a **Monotransitive Verb**. The verb is a Physical action.

12. The river ran dry

    - *ran* is a **Light Unaccusative Verb and Linking Verb**
    - *dry* is the **Subject Resultative Complement** 

13. The milk went sour

    - *went* is a **Light Unaccusative Verb and Linking Verb**
    - *sour* is **Subject Resultative Complement**

14. The milk went into the fridge

    - *went* is an **Intransitive Verb**
    - "*into the fridge*" is a **Place Locative Complement** and a **NP**

15. It explains a lot

    - *explains* is a **Monotransitive Verb**
    - "*a lot*" is the **DO, Pronoun and Phrasal Quantifier**

16. It explains a lot of things

    - *explains* is a **Monotransitive Verb**
    - "*a lot of things*" is the **DO and NP**
    - "*a lot of*" is a **Central Determiner and Phrasal Quantifier**

17. It explains so much

    - *explains* is a **Monotransitive Verb**
    - "*so much*" is a **Quantifier Phrase, a Pronoun and DO**. "*so*" is an **Adverb of Degree** that acts as an **intensifier**.

18. A lot of things are missing

    - "*A lot of things*" is the **subject and NP**
    - *are* is a **Linking Verb**
    - *missing* is an **Adjective and Subject Complement**
    - "*A lot of*" is a **Phrasal Quantifier, and a Central Determiner**

19. A lot of stuff is missing

    - *is* is agreeing with the head word of the NP "*stuff*"

20. It explains a great deal of things

    - *explains* is a **Monotransitive Verb**
    - "*a great deal of things*" is the **DO and NP**
    - "*a great deal of*" is a **Phrasal Quantifier and Central Determiner**

21. A great deal of information is missing

    - "*A great deal of information*" is the **Subject and NP**
    - *is* is agreeing with **information**

22. There is lots of noise.

    - *There* is the **Expletive Pronoun, the Grammatical Subject** (*a Dummy/Placeholder*)
    - *is* is a **Linking Verb**, agreeing with the **Notional Subject** (noise)
    - *noise* is the **Notional Subject or True Subject**

23. The car is making strange noises.

    - "*is making*" is a **Progressive Verb Phrase**
    - *making* is **Monotransitive**
    - *strange* is an **Attributive Adjective**

24. I have something that I can sit next to

    - *have* is a **Monotransitive Verb**
    - "*something [clause]*" is the **DO and NP** and the [*clause*] is a **Relative Clause or Adjective Clause**
    - *that* is a **Relative Pronoun** (which replaces a noun, it is standing in for "*something*") and **Object of the Preposition "next to"**
    - "*next to*" is a **Complex Preposition** and a **Stranded Preposition**

25. I have something to sit next to.

    - *have* is a **Monotransitive Verb**
    - "*something [phrase]*" is the **DO and NP** and the *phrase* is an **Adjectival Post-Modifier**
    - "*to sit*" is an **Infinitive Phrase**
    - *next to* is a **Complex Stranded Preposition**

26. I gave him a book to read

    - *gave* is a **Ditransitive Verb**
    - *him* is the IO
    - "*a book to read*" is the **DO and NP**
    - *book* is the **head word of the NP**
    - "*to read*" is an **Infinitive Phrase and an Adjectival Post Modifier**. Also, its **agent is him, and its object is "a book"**

27. I need a tool to work with

    - *need* is a **Monotransitive Verb**
    - "*a tool [Phrase]*" is the **DO and NP**, and the *Phrase* is an **Adjectival Post-Modifier and Infinitive Phrase**
    - "*to work*" is the **Infinitive Verb**, and its *agent* is **"I"**
    - *with* is a **Stranded Preposition** and its *object* is "**a tool**"

28. She found a place to stay at.

    - *found* is a **Monotransitive Version**
    - "*a place [phrase]*" is the **DO and NP**. The *Phrase* is an **Adjectival Post-Modifier and an Infinitive Phrase**
    - "*to stay*" is an **Infinitive Verb and head of the Infinitive Phrase**. Its *agent* is **She**
    - *at* is a **Stranded Preposition**. Its *object* is "**a place**"

29. The subject is provided with an object to act upon.

    - "*The subject*" is the **Subject and NP**
    - "*is provided*" is a **Passive Verb Phrase**
    - "*with an object [Phrase]*" is a **Prepositional Phrase**. This is a **Prepositional Complement of the verb "provide"**. The verb "provide" often takes the pattern "*be provided with something*". The Phrase is an **Adjectival Post-Modifier and Infinitive Phrase**.
    - "*to act*" is an **Infinitive Verb**. Its *agent* is "**The subject**"
    - *upon* is a **Stranded Preposition**. Its *object* is "**an object**".
