# Sentences

1. Tough choices make heroes brave.
   - *make* is a Complex Transitive Verb.
   - *brave* is an **Object Complement, a Resultative, Dynamic adjective**

2. I painted the walls yellow.
   - *painted* is a Complex Transitive Verb.
   - *yellow* is an **Object Complement, a Resultative, Stative adjective**

3. I found the walls yellow.
   - The analysis can be done in 2 ways.
     - If you mean "I judged them", then *found* is a **Complex Transitive Verb** and *yellow* is an **Object Complement** and an **Depictive Complement**.
     - If you mean "I located them", then *found* is a **Monotransitive Verb** and yellow is a **Physical Depictive Adjunct**.

4. I found it boring

   - *found* is **CTV**
   - *boring* is an **OC and a Depictive Complement**

5. He ate the pizza cold

   - *ate* is a **Monotransitive Verb**
   - "*the pizza*" is the **DO**
   - *cold* is a **Physical Object-Oriented Depictive Adjunct**

6. I found the dog alive

   - The analysis can be done in 2 ways.
     - If you mean "I located the dog (and it happened to be alive)", then *found* is **Monotransitive** and *alive* is a **Depictive Adjunct**
     - If you mean "I discovered that the dog was alive", then *found* is a **CTV** and *alive* is a **Depictive Complement**

7. She left the door open.
   - *left* is a Complex Transitive Verb and it functions as a **causative/stative bridge**.
   - *open* is the **Object Complement**, and a Resultative and Stative.

8. The new rules made my job harder.

   - *made* is a **CTV**.
   - *harder* is the **Object Complement**, a **Resultative and a Comparative adjective**.
   - *my* is a **Central Possessive Determiner**.

11. He made me clean my room.

    - *made* is a **Complex Transitive Verb**
    - *me* is the Direct Object
    - "*clean my room*" is the **Object Complement and Verb Phrase** (**Bare Infinitive**)

12. I watched the sun setting.

    - *watched* is a **CTV**. Specifically, **a Verb of Perception**.
    - "*the sun*" is a **Direct Object** and **Noun Phrase**.
    - *setting* is the **Object Complement and a Present Participle**
      - Since you are using the Present Participle instead of the Past Participle (*I watched the sun set*), the focus is **on the process of the sun going down**, not just the finished result. Also, this form suggests you caught it **in the middle** of the action and did not watch it from top to bottom.

13. "I saw him cleaning his room." and "I saw him clean his room" 
    - Both sentences are correct and their analysis is pretty similar, the only difference is that the first sentence uses a **Present Participle Verb Phrase as an Object Complement**; meanwhile the second sentences uses a **Bare Infinitive Phrase as an Object Complement**.

14. "I saw the room clean." and "I saw the room cleaned"
    - Both sentences are correct, but the meaning and semantic change a bit.
      In both sentences, the verb *saw* is a **CTV**, but the **Object Complement part is different**
      - *clean* is a pure adjective, in this case, the **Object Complement is a Physical Depictive Complement, it is a Stative Adjective**. 
        The sentence is equivalent to "I found the room clean".
        You perceived a State.
      - *cleaned* is a **Passive Action, a Past Participle**.
        The passive part (-ed) captures that "acted-upon" quality, so someone *cleaned* the room. 
        Unlike the adjective *clean* (which is just a quality), *cleaned* implies that the event of cleaning has taken place. The process was done to the room.
        You perceived a Passive Action, the result of it.

15. A door opens in your mind when you learn this.

    - *opens* is an **Unaccusative Verb**, a **Verb of Motion/Change**.  It is also an **Ergative verb**, the **Intransitive (Unaccusative)** side of this verb.
    - "*in your mind*" is a **Verb Adjunct**, and **Adjunct of Place** (**Locative**). It is also a **Prepositional Phrase**.
    - "*when you learn this*" is a **Temporal Subordinate Clause**
      - *when* is the **Subordinating Conjunction**. It links the dependent clause to the main clause.
      - *you* is the **Subject/pronoun**
      - *learn* is a **Monotransitive Verb**.
      - *this* is the Direct Object and Demonstrative Pronoun. The thing being learned.

    <u>Note</u>: A note for "*in your mind*". The phrase is a **Verb Adjunct**, it is not mandatory. Compare this to a **Verb Complement** (specifically a **Locative Complement**), where the location is mandatory for the verb to function:

    - I put the book **on the table**. 
      *Test*: "I put the book" (This fails; the sentence is incomplete because *put* requires a location)

    <u>Note</u>: A note for *Adjuncts*. **Adjuncts of Place or Time** are used often:

    - The meeting started **at 9:00 AM**. (*Adjunct of Time*)

    - The equipment was installed **at the site**. (*Adjunct of Place*)

      And because they're *adjuncts*, you can move them around for emphasis:

    - **At the site**, the equipment was installed (This still works because adjuncts are flexible!)

16. The mineral deposits reside under the surface.

    - *The mineral deposits* is the subject and NP.
      - *The* is the central determiner
      - mineral is an **Attributive Adjective**. It modifies deposits.
    - *reside* is an **Intransitive Verb**. Specifically, it's a **Verb of Residence/Position**.
    - "*under the surface*" is a **Locative Complement** (A type of **Verb Complement**). It is also a **Prepositional Phrase**.

17. The workers placed the equipment inside the shaft.

    - "*inside the shaft*" is a **Locative Complement**, a **PP** and an **Adverbial of Place** (*Functional label*).
    - *placed* is a **CTV**.

18. The workers repaired the equipment inside the shaft.

    - *repaired* is a **Monotransitive Verb**.
    - *the equipment* is the **Direct Object**.
    - *inside the shaft* is a **Locative Adjunct** (*Structural label*), a **Prepositional Phrase** and an **Adverbial of Place** (*Functional label*)

19. The workers extracted the ore carefully last night

    - *extracted* is a **Monotransitive Verb**.
    - *carefully* is a **Verb Adjunct** (*Structural*) and an **Adverbial of Manner** (*Functional*).
    - "*last night*" is also a **Verb Adjunct** (*Structural*) and an **Adverbial of Time** (*Functional*). It is also a **Noun Phrase**.
      - *last* is a **post-determiner/adjective**
      - *night* is the head of the **NP**.

    <u>Note</u>: **Ordinals** like *first, last, next* or **Cardinals** like *one, two* come after the central determiner but before standard descriptive adjectives, so they fill out the position of **Post-determiner**.

20. The workers extracted the ore during the night shift.

    - *extracted* is a **Monotransitive Verb**
    - "*during the night shift*" is a **Verb Adjunct**, an **Adverbial of Time and a PP**
    - *night* is a **Attributive Noun** or **Noun Adjunct**

21. The shift lasted until midnight.

    - *lasted* is an **Intransitive Verb**.
    - "*until midnight*" is a **Temporal Locative Complement**, **Prepositional Phrase** and **Adverbial of Time**

22. The supervisor kept the records in the office

    - *kept* is a **CTV**
    - "*in the office*" is a **Locative Complement, a Place Locative, and a Prepositional Phrase**, and an **Adverbial of Place**.

23. He baked the bread brown.

    - *baked* is a **CTV**.
    - *brown* is a **Resultative Adjective** and **Object Complement**.

   

21. Look at the room.
    - The sentence is **Imperative**. The subject "*You*" is implied (the *hidden* subject)
    - "*Look at*" is a **Transitive Phrasal Verb** (specifically a **Prepositional Verb**). It is inseparable

22. All the workers extracted the ore.
    - "*All the workers*" is the **subject and NP**
    - *All* is a **Pre-Determiner**, *the* is a **Central Determiner**, *workers* is the **head of the NP**

23. All of the workers extracted the ore.
    - "*All of the workers*" is the **Subject, NP and a Quantifying Partitive**
    - *All* is the **head of the NP, a pronoun and a quantifier**
    - "*of the workers*" is an **Adjectival Post-Modifier and a PP**

24. Two tons of ore were processed.

    - "*Two tons of ore*" is the **Subject, NP and Measure Partitive**
      - *Two* is a **Central Determiner** (specifically a **Cardinal Numeral**), and a **Pre-Modifier** (Quantifies the Head)
      - *tons* is the **Head of the NP**
      - "*of ore*" is a **PP and an Adjectival Post-Modifier**
    - "*were processed*" is a **Passive Verb Phrase**
      - *were* is the **Auxiliary Verb** (Passive Marker)
      - *processed* is the **Past Participle** (Main verb)

    <u>Note</u>: There's a rule:
    If you said "The two tons", then *the* is the **Central Determiner** and *two* is a **Post-Determiner**. But since *Two* is standing alone at the front, it takes the Central slot.

25. A number of miners are waiting.

    - "*A number of*" is a **Phrasal Quantifier and Central Determiner**.
    - *are* is an **Auxiliary Verb**
    - *waiting* is an **Intransitive Verb**

26. The number of miners is 50.

    - *The* is a **Central Determiner**
    - *number* is the **head of the NP**
    - "*of miners*" is a **PP, and an Adjectival Post-Modifier**
    - *50* is a **Noun and Subject Complement**

27. This is where I was last time

    - *This* is a **Demonstrative Pronoun**.
    - *is* is a **Linking Verb**.
    - "*where I was last time*" is a **Noun Clause and a Subject Complement**
    - *where* is the **Subordinating Conjunction**, but also it is a **Relative Adverb** (*it represents the location*) or **Adverb of Place** (*fronted*)
    - *was* is <span type="incorrect">NOT a Linking Verb</span>, but an **Intransitive (Locative) Verb.**
    - "*last time*" is an **Adverbial of Time, an Adverb Adjunct and a NP**

    <u>Note</u>: Usually, "*was*" is a linking verb (e.g., "*I was tired*"). But when *was* means "**to be located/present**", it is **Intransitive** because it isn't linking the subject to an adjective; it's just stating existence in a place.

28. The tired workers in the office are drinking water.

    - *The* is the **Central Determiner** and **Pre-Modifier**
    - *tired* is an **Attributive Adjective** and a **Pre-Modifier**
    - "*in the office*" is an **Adjectival Post-Modifier and PP**

    <u>Note</u>: If the sentence were "The workers are drinking water **in the office**", then the bold part would be **Adverbial of Place**. But by placing it inside the Subject NP, it is a modifier of the noun.

29. I had the report translated

    - *had* is a **Complex Transitive Verb**, meaning "**to keep/possess in a state**"
    - "*the report*" is the DO and NP
    -  *translated* is the **Object Complement** and a **Resultative Adjective**

    <u>Note</u>: The structure of *have* could've been to use a **verb** as an **Object Complement**. In this case, the meaning would've changed a bit to "I caused the report to be translated"

30. I want the mineral samples tested.

    - *want* is a **CTV**
    - *tested* is an **OC** and a **Resultative Adjective**

31. The manager wants the project finished

    - *wants* is a **CTV**
    - *finished* is a **OC and a Resultative Adjective**

32. You have the structure mapped

    - *have* is a **CTV** indicating possession of a state
    - "*the structure*" is the **DO and a NP**
    - *mapped* is an **Object Complement, a Resultative Adjective**
    - The sentence means **I have the structure + it is mapped**.

33. He bought his brother a car.

    - *bought* is a **Ditransitive Verb**
    - "*his brother*" is the **IO and NP**
    - "a car" is the **DO and NP**

34. He bought a car for his brother

    - *bought* is a **Monotransitive Verb**
    - "*a car*" is the **DO and NP**
    - "*for his brother*" is a **Verb Adjunct, an Adverbial of Beneficiary and a PP**

35. The food turned bad

    - "*The food*" is the **Subject, NP and Patient/Undergoer**
    - *turned* is a **Linking Verb and a Light Unaccusative Verb** (It is light because it needs the adjective)
      - The logic is that the food didn't *turn* (like a car turns a corner); the food **underwent a transition** into the state of being "bad"
    - *bad* is the **Subject Complement, Resultative Adjective Complement**

36. He turned the page

    - *turned* is a **Monotransitive Verb**. The verb is a Physical action.

37. The river ran dry

    - *ran* is a **Light Unaccusative Verb and Linking Verb**
    - *dry* is the **Subject Resultative Complement** 

38. The milk went sour

    - *went* is a **Light Unaccusative Verb and Linking Verb**
    - *sour* is **Subject Resultative Complement**

39. The milk went into the fridge

    - *went* is an **Intransitive Verb**
    - "*into the fridge*" is a **Place Locative Complement** and a **NP**

40. It explains a lot

    - *explains* is a **Monotransitive Verb**
    - "*a lot*" is the **DO, Pronoun and Phrasal Quantifier**

41. It explains a lot of things

    - *explains* is a **Monotransitive Verb**
    - "*a lot of things*" is the **DO and NP**
    - "*a lot of*" is a **Central Determiner and Phrasal Quantifier**

42. It explains so much

    - *explains* is a **Monotransitive Verb**
    - "*so much*" is a **Quantifier Phrase, a Pronoun and DO**. "*so*" is an **Adverb of Degree** that acts as an **intensifier**.

43. A lot of things are missing

    - "*A lot of things*" is the **subject and NP**
    - *are* is a **Linking Verb**
    - *missing* is an **Adjective and Subject Complement**
    - "*A lot of*" is a **Phrasal Quantifier, and a Central Determiner**

44. A lot of stuff is missing

    - *is* is agreeing with the head word of the NP "*stuff*"

45. It explains a great deal of things

    - *explains* is a **Monotransitive Verb**
    - "*a great deal of things*" is the **DO and NP**
    - "*a great deal of*" is a **Phrasal Quantifier and Central Determiner**

46. A great deal of information is missing

    - "*A great deal of information*" is the **Subject and NP**
    - *is* is agreeing with **information**

47. There is lots of noise.

    - *There* is the **Expletive Pronoun, the Grammatical Subject** (*a Dummy/Placeholder*)
    - *is* is a **Linking Verb**, agreeing with the **Notional Subject** (noise)
    - *noise* is the **Notional Subject or True Subject**

48. The car is making strange noises.

    - "*is making*" is a **Progressive Verb Phrase**
    - *making* is **Monotransitive**
    - *strange* is an **Attributive Adjective**

49. I have something that I can sit next to

    - *have* is a **Monotransitive Verb**
    - "*something [clause]*" is the **DO and NP** and the [*clause*] is a **Relative Clause or Adjective Clause**
    - *that* is a **Relative Pronoun** (which replaces a noun, it is standing in for "*something*") and **Object of the Preposition "next to"**
    - "*next to*" is a **Complex Preposition** and a **Stranded Preposition**

50. I have something to sit next to.

    - *have* is a **Monotransitive Verb**
    - "*something [phrase]*" is the **DO and NP** and the *phrase* is an **Adjectival Post-Modifier**
    - "*to sit*" is an **Infinitive Phrase**
    - *next to* is a **Complex Stranded Preposition**

51. I gave him a book to read

    - *gave* is a **Ditransitive Verb**
    - *him* is the IO
    - "*a book to read*" is the **DO and NP**
    - *book* is the **head word of the NP**
    - "*to read*" is an **Infinitive Phrase and an Adjectival Post Modifier**. Also, its **agent is him, and its object is "a book"**

52. I need a tool to work with

    - *need* is a **Monotransitive Verb**
    - "*a tool [Phrase]*" is the **DO and NP**, and the *Phrase* is an **Adjectival Post-Modifier and Infinitive Phrase**
    - "*to work*" is the **Infinitive Verb**, and its *agent* is **"I"**
    - *with* is a **Stranded Preposition** and its *object* is "**a tool**"

53. She found a place to stay at.

    - *found* is a **Monotransitive Version**
    - "*a place [phrase]*" is the **DO and NP**. The *Phrase* is an **Adjectival Post-Modifier and an Infinitive Phrase**
    - "*to stay*" is an **Infinitive Verb and head of the Infinitive Phrase**. Its *agent* is **She**
    - *at* is a **Stranded Preposition**. Its *object* is "**a place**"

54. The subject is provided with an object to act upon.

    - "*The subject*" is the **Subject and NP**
    - "*is provided*" is a **Passive Verb Phrase**
    - "*with an object [Phrase]*" is a **Prepositional Phrase**. This is a **Prepositional Complement of the verb "provide"**. The verb "provide" often takes the pattern "*be provided with something*". The Phrase is an **Adjectival Post-Modifier and Infinitive Phrase**.
    - "*to act*" is an **Infinitive Verb**. Its *agent* is "**The subject**"
    - *upon* is a **Stranded Preposition**. Its *object* is "**an object**".
