1. A door opens in your mind when you learn this.

   - A door: Subject and NP

   - opens: Intransitive Verb, an Unaccusative Verb (Verb of Motion/Change). It is also an Ergative Verb, the Intransitive (Unaccusative) side of the verb.

   - in your mind: Verb Adjunct, and Adverbial of Place, and a Prepositional Phrase
     - in: head word of the PP
     - your mind: Object of the Preposition

   - when you learn this: Temporal Subordinate Clause, and an Adverbial of Time
     - when: Subordinating Conjunction, linking the dependent clause to the main clause
     - you: Subject and Pronoun
     - learn: Monotransitive Verb
     - this: DO and Demonstrative Pronoun.
2. The food turned bad/poisonous
   - The food: Subject and NP
   - turned: Linking Verb and CIV
   - bad/poisonous: Subject Complement and Resultative Adjective Phrase
4. He turned traitor
   - turned: CIV
   - traitor: Subject Complement and NP 
   - turned has a special characteristic. When it is used to mean a change in **allegiance, profession, or religion**, it deletes the article. Similar: "She turned **doctor**", "He turned **Catholic**"
