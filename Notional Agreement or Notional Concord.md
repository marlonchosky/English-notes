# Notional Agreement (Notional Concord / Synesis)

**Notional Agreement** is a grammatical phenomenon where a verb matches the semantic meaning (the underlying notion) of a subject phrase, rather than strictly conforming to its surface morphosyntactic form. 

---

## 1. The "Look-Through" Mechanism (Quantifier Phrase Configurations)

When dealing with **indefinite quantifiers** like *all, some, half, fraction, or none*, the surface head of the noun phrase is technically a partitive pronoun. However, these pronouns do not possess inherent, fixed number features (singular or plural). 

To resolve agreement, the finite verb utilizes a **"Look-Through" Mechanism**—it bypasses the structural quantifier head and copies its agreement features directly from the noun within the partitive *of*-phrase.

| Sentence Example                  | Structural Head            | Notional Core Noun             | Verb Agreement    |
| :-------------------------------- | :------------------------- | :----------------------------- | :---------------- |
| *All of the **water** is gone.*   | *All* (Indefinite Pronoun) | **water** (Singular Mass Noun) | **is** (Singular) |
| *All of the **miners** are safe.* | *All* (Indefinite Pronoun) | **miners** (Plural Count Noun) | **are** (Plural)  |

---

## 2. Structural Contrast: With "Of" vs. Without "Of"

A common point of confusion is why structures with and without the preposition *of* yield the exact same verb agreement, despite having completely different underlying syntactic configurations.

### Configuration A: With "Of" (True Notional Agreement)
* **Sentence:** *All of the water **is** gone.*
* **Syntactic Blueprint:** `Quantifier Head [All] + Partitive Post-Modifier [of the water] + Verb [is]`
* **The Logic:** The verb is forced to look through the empty number features of the indefinite pronoun head *All* to find the mass noun *water*, triggering a **singular** verb via the look-through mechanism.

### Configuration B: Without "Of" (Canonical Formal Agreement)
* **Sentence:** *All the water **is** gone.*
* **Syntactic Blueprint:** `[Pre-Determiner: All] + [Central Determiner: The] + Head Noun: [water] + Verb [is]`
* **The Logic:** Here, there is no partitive pronoun loop. According to modern DP syntax, *all* and *the* operate purely as functional specifiers turning the phrase into a Determiner Phrase (DP). The word **water** is the literal, structural head noun of the entire subject phrase. The verb agrees with it automatically via standard **Formal Agreement**, not notional agreement.

---

## 3. Collective Nouns (The Ultimate Notional Shift)

Another major domain of Notional Concord occurs with collective nouns (*the team, the staff, the crew, committee*). Here, the surface noun is indisputably singular, but the verb can toggle number features based on speaker perspective:

* **Formal Concord (Focus on the Unified Entity):** *The committee **has** issued its report.* ($\rightarrow$ The committee acts as one singular, institutional unit).
* **Notional Concord (Focus on Individual Members):** *The committee **have** disagreed on the strategy.* ($\rightarrow$ The verb looks through the singular noun to target the individual human actors within the group who are acting independently).
