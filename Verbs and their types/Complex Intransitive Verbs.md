# Description

While **Complex Transitive Verbs (CTV)** focus on the **Object**, **Complex Intransitive Verbs (CIV)** focus entirely on the **Subject**.

The first half of CIVs uses a **Subject Complement** to describe *what* the subject is. They are usually called **Linking Verbs**.

The second half uses a **Locative Complement** to describe *where* the subject is.

---

# Copular CIV or Linking Verb

**Structure**: ***Subject + Verb + Subject Complement*** (<span type="group-1">***S = Complement***</span>)

## Verbs of Being/Identity (The "Equal Sign")

These state what the subject *is*.

- **Verbs**: be, become, remain, stay.
- **Example**: He **became** a senior developer. (***He = Developer***)



## Verbs of Perception (The "Appearance")

These state how the subject *appears* to the senses.

- **Verbs:** seem, appear, look, sound, smell, feel, taste.

- **Example:** The logic **sounds** correct."(***Logic = Correct***).



## Verbs of Change/Result (The "Transition")

These describe a subject moving into a new state.

- **Verbs:** grow, turn, get, fall.
- **Example:** The weather **turned** cold. (***Weather = Cold***).

---

# Locative CIV

**Structure**: ***Subject + Verb + Locative Complement*** (<span type="group-1">***S → Place***</span>)

- **Verbs**: reside, last, be

- **Examples**:
  
  - The deposits **reside** [underground].
  
  - The shift **lasted** [until midnight].
  - The server **is** [in the rack]
