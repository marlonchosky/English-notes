# Verb Adjuncts

An **Adjunct** is any part of a sentence that is not required by the verb. You can "delete" them, and the sentence remains grammatically structural.

We have these:

1. Adverbial Adjunct
2. Predicative Adjunct (**Secondary CIVs**)
3. Prepositional Adjunct

---

# 1. Adverbial Adjunct

The **Action** (How, When, Where).

- The river froze **quickly**
- The river froze **at night**.
- He ate the pizza **hungrily**

---

# 2. Predicative Adjunct (Secondary Predicate) (<span type="group-2">Secondary CIVs</span>)

The **Noun's State** (Who, What) is a Predicative.

- The river froze **solid**.

  - The river [is] solid (This is the hidden **Secondary Predicate**)
  - froze: Unaccusative and Secondary CIV
  - solid: Secondary Predicate / Resultative Predicative Adjunct, and an Adjective Phrase.
- The river glowed **blue**

  - The river [was] blue

  - glowed: Unaccusative and Secondary CIV
  - blue: Secondary Predicate / Depictive Predicative Adjunct, and an Adjective Phrase
- He ate the pizza **cold**.

  - The pizza [was] cold.
  - ate: Monotransitive Verb
  - cold: Secondary Predicate / Depictive Predicative Adjunct (Object-Oriented), and an Adjective Phrase
- The vase broke **into pieces**

  - broke: Unaccusative and Secondary CIV
  - The vase [is now] in pieces
  - into pieces: Resultative Predicative Adjunct / Secondary Predicate and Prepositional Phrase 
- He broke the vase **into pieces**
  - broke: Monotransitive Verb
  - the vase: DO and NP
  - into pieces: Secondary Predicate / Resultative Predicative Adjunct, and a PP
    - Secondary Predicate: The vase is in pieces
- He died **a hero**.
  - He died a hero
    - died: Unaccusative and Secondary CIV
    - a hero: Secondary Predicate and Depictive Predicative Adjunct, and NP
      - Secondary Predicate: He was a hero
- He wiped the table **to a shine**.
  - wiped: Monotransitive Verb
  - the table: DO and NP
  - to a shine: Secondary Predicate and Resultative Predicative Adjunct, and a PP
    - Secondary Predicate: The table is shiny
    - to: head word of the PP
    - a shine: Object of the Preposition and NP
- He beat the metal **into a flat sheet** 

  - The metal [is] a flat sheet (the PP becomes a NP)

So, the Secondary Predicate or Predicative Adjunct can be

1. Noun Phrases
2. Adjectival Phrases
3. Prepositional Phrases

and also it can be **Resultative or Depictive**.

---

# 3. Prepositional Adjunct

- He bought a car **for his brother**.
  - bought: Monotransitive Verb
  - a car: DO and NP
  - for his brother: **Prepositional Adjunct** and PP
