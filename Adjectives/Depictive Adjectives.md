# Depictive Secondary Predicates (Depictive Adjectives)

A **depictive adjective** (often called a *depictive secondary predicate*) describes the **state** or **condition** of the subject or the object *at the time* the main action takes place.

Unlike standard attributive adjectives (e.g., "the *cold* pizza"), depictives do not just modify the noun; they describe a temporary state during an event.

## 1. Depictive Adjuncts

An **adjunct** is optional information. If you remove it, the sentence still makes perfect grammatical sense.

### Subject-Oriented Depictive Adjuncts

These describe the state of the **subject** performing the action.

- **Corrected Example:** *He licked the plate **hungry**.* (Or: *He returned home **angry**.*)
  - *Note on your original:* "He licked the plate *angry*" works structurally, but using "hungry" or "empty-handed" sounds more natural. If you mean *how* he licked it (viciously/madly), you would use an adverb (*angrily*). If you use the adjective *angry*, it means "He was angry while he licked the plate."
  - **Grammar Breakdown:** *licked* is a **Monotransitive Verb**, *the plate* is the Direct Object, and *hungry/angry* is a **Subject-Oriented Depictive Adjunct**.

### Object-Oriented Depictive Adjuncts

These describe the state of the **direct object** during the action.

- **Example:** *I ate the pizza **cold**.*
  - **Grammar Breakdown:** *ate* is a **Monotransitive Verb**, and *cold* is an **Object-Oriented Depictive Adjunct**. (The pizza was cold when I ate it; I didn't heat it up).

## 2. Depictive Complements

A **complement** is *required* by the verb to complete the meaning of the sentence. If you remove it, the sentence changes meaning entirely or becomes ungrammatical. These occur with **Complex Transitive Verbs**.

- **Example:** *I found the walls **yellow**.*
  - **Meaning A (Mental Judgment/Evaluation):** "I discovered/judged the walls to be yellow."
  - **Meaning B (Result of a search):** "I searched for the walls, and when I located them, they were yellow."
  - **Grammar Breakdown:** *found* is a **Complex Transitive Verb**, and *yellow* is an **Object Depictive Complement**.

> ⚠️ **Complement vs. Adjunct Tip:** > * *I ate the pizza cold.* → *I ate the pizza.* (Sentence still makes total sense. → **Adjunct**)
>
> - *I found the walls yellow.* → *I found the walls.* (Completely changes the meaning of "found" from *judged* to *located*. → **Complement**)

## The Two Types of Depictives

1. **Physical Depictives:** Describe a literal, physical state of the noun.
   - *Example:* "She drank the coffee **hot**." / "He fell asleep **clothed**."
2. **Evaluative/Mental Depictives:** Describe a psychological state, emotional state, or a value judgment.
   - *Example:* "They married **young**." / "She judge the movie **awful**."
